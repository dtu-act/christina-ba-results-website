Abstract

Acoustic simulation has shown to be a valuable tool for creating realistic virtual reality environments and developing and improving acoustic conditions. A commonly used, and often favoured, simulation method based on geometrical acoustics show inaccuracy in small spaces and with low frequencies. Another method using waveequation solvers yields accurate results but is often deselected due to expensive computations. By combining the two methods the advantages from both simulations can be gained. If a hybrid is constructed, at which frequency should the two simulations meet? The purpose of the project is to find the lowest cut-off frequency that perceptually gives little to no audible difference between signals created in the two different simulations. The frequency is determined based on a listening experiment tested on 9 test participants. The test will be simulated using different room conditions to investigate the impact of geometry on the cross-over frequency.

Conditions

There are three different room types; a rectangular room, L-shaped room and studio. All rooms have absorption coefficient 0.3227 and scattering coefficient 0.05.
